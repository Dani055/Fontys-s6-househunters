All of the production instances of the project's microservices operate under low-privilege accounts. They are specifically created with just enough privileges so that the application can be operational, while also limiting damage in case of a security breach. The accounts are currently 2 and are related to the production database and s3 bucket.

The production database is connected to using a production account that has access only to the 3 databases used, with allowed operations being:

  • Read

  • Write (within the database, drop not allowed)

A screenshot of the account from MongoDB atlas:

The production S3 bucket is also managed by a low-privilege user. The only allowed actions for the user are read/write to that specific bucket


For google cloud deployment, my pipeline also takes advantage of a low-privilege service account. This account is limited to read/write only for the specific cluster it deploys to and does not have access to any of the cloud compute/VPC components of GCloud, since they are configured only once on first deployment and are not needed for consecutive deployments
